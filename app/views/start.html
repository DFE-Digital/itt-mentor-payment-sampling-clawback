Use this service to claim payment for the time a teacher spends becoming an initial teacher training (ITT) mentor, also known as a 'general mentor'. Payments will be made directly to the school or placement organisation.

{# -------- SECTION --------- #}

Who can use this service

To use this service, your school or placement organisation must:

  • offer ITT placements with an approved provider
  • be registered with Get Information about Schools (GIAS)
  • have at least one teacher who has started, or is due to start, their ITT mentor (general mentor) training this academic year

This funding is not available for training early career framework (ECF) mentors. To claim for ECF mentors, you need a different service.

{# -------- SECTION --------- #}

When to submit a claim

To submit a claim, you first need to register your mentors.

Key dates:

  • You can submit a claim from spring 2025
  • For training that took place from April 2024 for the school year starting September 2025, you must wait until May 2025 to submit a claim
  • have at least one teacher who has started, or is due to start, their ITT mentor (general mentor) training this academic year
{# -------- SECTION --------- #}

Before you start

To start a claim, you will be asked to add your mentors to the service.

To add your mentors, you will need:

  • a DfE Sign-in account
  • the name of your ITT accredited provider
  • your mentors' teacher reference number (TRN)
  • your mentors' dates of birth

To submit a claim, you need to add the number of training hours mentors have completed – this should match your provider's.

Start now {# -------- SECTION --------- #}

How much can I claim

You can claim different amounts depending on where your school or placement organisation is located. You can claim up to:

  • £876 outside of London
  • £1072 inside London
{# -------- SECTION --------- #}

How many hours can I claim for

A claim can be submitted for between 1 to 20 hours for each mentor. The service will only show the maximum hours a mentor can claim. For example, if a mentor is completing their 6 hours of top-up training, the service will only show the available 6 hours.

The mentor does not need to complete the full 20 hours to be eligible for payment.

When will I receive payment

Payment will be made directly to the placement school by the Education and Skills Funding Agency (ESFA) or from your Local Authority. Once approved, payment will be made from October following your claim submission date.
